# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Indian families and individuals across all life stages making insurance decisions:

- Young families (28-35) buying their first life or health insurance
- Mid-career professionals (35-50) reviewing comprehensive coverage
- Business owners renewing motor policies or seeking group health insurance
- Pre-retirees and retirees (50+) looking for secure investment options

**Context:** Users are researching insurance options, comparing advisors, and deciding whether to trust NKT with their financial security. Most prefer phone consultation before committing. They're browsing from mobile during commute or desktop at home/office. Decision-making is careful, not impulsive.

**Job to be done:** Find a trustworthy insurance advisor who can explain options clearly, get a quote or estimate, and initiate contact (phone/WhatsApp) for personalized consultation.

## Product Purpose

NKT Insurance Solutions is a lead-generation website for an independent insurance advisor specializing in Life (LIC), Health, and Motor insurance in India.

**Why it exists:** To convert website visitors into qualified leads by building trust, demonstrating expertise, and making it easy to request consultation. Every section should move visitors toward filling the lead form or initiating WhatsApp contact.

**Success looks like:** High-quality leads (name + phone number + insurance interest) that convert to policy sales through phone consultation.

## Brand Personality

**Trust · Warmth · Approachable · Clear**

**Voice:** The trusted neighborhood advisor you've known for 10 years. Personal, honest, conversational. Explains complex insurance simply without jargon. Calm confidence, not aggressive sales tactics.

**Tone:** Warm and human, never corporate or sterile. Professional without being distant. Helpful without being pushy.

**Emotional goals:**

- **Trust & Security** - Visitors feel this person/company will protect their family
- **Warmth & Personal** - Feels like talking to a friend, not a corporation
- **Professional & Expert** - Confidence that NKT knows insurance inside-out
- **Modern & Easy** - Simple process, no bureaucratic hassle

**NOT:** Premium/exclusive, tech-forward, aggressive, or impersonal.

## Anti-references

**What this should NOT look like:**

1. **PolicyBazaar and aggregator platforms** - Flashy, aggressive comparison tables, overwhelming choice paralysis, discount-focused, transactional feel. Too many CTAs screaming for attention. Gamified UI elements.

2. **Generic insurtech platforms** - Purple/blue gradients, SaaS dashboard aesthetic, over-engineered animations, "disrupting insurance" messaging, startup-y vibe that lacks gravitas for financial decisions.

3. **Large corporate insurance websites** - Sterile, impersonal, bureaucratic navigation, jargon-heavy copy, intimidating forms, no human face/personality, feels like dealing with a call center.

4. **AI-generated insurance templates** - Same card grid patterns, emoji icons (🛡️ ❤️ 🚗), identical "Why Choose Us" sections, formulaic "3 steps" process everywhere, no unique voice.

**The pattern:** Avoid anything that feels mass-market, transactional, overly corporate, or visually generic. Stand out by being personal, warm, and genuinely helpful.

## Design Principles

1. **Human first, not digital first** - Every design choice should feel like it came from a person who cares, not an algorithm. Real photos of people, conversational copy, personal advisor presence.

2. **Clarity over cleverness** - Insurance is already complex. The website should make decisions easier, not showcase design tricks. Clear hierarchy, plain language, obvious next steps.

3. **Earn trust before asking** - Build credibility through expertise, credentials, testimonials, and transparency before pushing form fills. Guide, don't pressure.

4. **Accessible to all** - Readable text sizes, high contrast, simple navigation, mobile-first thinking. If a grandmother or first-time internet user can't use it, redesign it.

5. **Local, not generic** - Reflect Indian context (₹ currency, local trust signals like IRDAI licensing, phone/WhatsApp preference, family-focused messaging) rather than global insurance template patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

**WCAG Level:** AA minimum (4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text)

**Key considerations:**

- Large touch targets (minimum 44x44px) for mobile-first audience
- Clear focus indicators for keyboard navigation
- Readable font sizes (17px minimum body text)
- Reduced motion alternatives for all animations
- Form validation with clear error messages in plain language
- Support for screen readers with proper ARIA labels
- Color is never the only indicator of information

**Known user needs:**

- Many users have limited English proficiency - keep copy simple and direct
- Mobile data can be slow/expensive - optimize images and minimize load
- Users may have low vision or use reading glasses - prioritize readability
- Phone calls are preferred over forms - make phone numbers prominent and tappable
